Many bacterial species are not well studied, and many more remain unknown to science. Our lab has partnered with Improbale Beef to study bacteria that have that ability to synthesize proteins that taste like beef. Labs across the country have had success in synthesizing meat from algae, fungi and microorganisms found on plant roots, but Improbable Beef is still searching for the microorganism that will synthesize the perfect taste.

The human body is a source of thousands of types of bacteria and different parts of the body harbor different species. We hypothesize that the ideal microorganism to synthesize may be found in the belly button - or at least in someone's belly button.

The dashboards below displays the most common bacterial species, by count, in the navels of the volunteers in the study. Volunteers can use the dashboard to assess whether they are eligible to sell their navel bacteria to Improbable Beef.
